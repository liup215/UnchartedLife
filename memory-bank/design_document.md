## Legends of Uncharted Life Detailed Design

### Refocused Game Background
In a post-apocalyptic wasteland, traditional life forms are disappearing. The player, a biologically engineered researcher, must explore unknown life forms, understand biological principles, and rebuild the miracle of life through genetic engineering and biotechnology.

### Biology Knowledge System

#### Core Educational Content
**Cell Biology Module**
- Cell structure and function
- Cellular respiration and ATP generation
- Cell membrane transport mechanisms
- Cell division processes

**Genetics Module**
- DNA structure and replication
- Gene expression and regulation
- Genetic variation and evolution
- Genetic engineering techniques

**Ecology Module**
- Ecosystem structure
- Food chains and energy flow
- Population dynamics
- Biodiversity conservation

**Evolutionary Biology Module**
- Natural selection mechanisms
- Adaptive evolution
- Speciation processes
- Molecular evidence for evolution

#### Integration of Knowledge and Game Mechanics

**Glucose Energy System ↔ Cellular Respiration**
- Players need to understand glycolysis and the citric acid cycle.
- Improve ATP recovery efficiency through an "Optimize Cellular Respiration" quest.
- Upgrading the number of mitochondria directly affects energy recovery speed.

**Vehicle Bionics System ↔ Evolutionary Adaptation**
- Study the adaptive traits of different animals.
- Learn bionics principles to modify vehicles.
- Each bionic modification has a corresponding biological explanation.

**Genetic Modification System ↔ Genetic Engineering**
- Learn CRISPR technology through a gene-editing minigame.
- Understand gene expression regulation mechanisms.
- Unlock new biological abilities corresponding to real gene functions.

### Chapter Structure Design

#### Chapter 1: Cell Awakening
**Learning Objective:** Basic cell knowledge
**Plot:** The player awakens from a cryogenic pod and needs to reactivate their body's cellular functions.

**Level Design:**
1. **Tutorial: Metabolic Restart**
   - Learn ATP synthesis mechanisms.
   - Unlock basic energy recovery.
   - Basic vehicle operation.

2. **Exploration: The Cell Factory**
   - Explore an abandoned biological laboratory.
   - Learn about organelle functions.
   - Obtain materials for mitochondria upgrades.

3. **Combat: Membrane Wars**
   - Fight against virus-infected robots.
   - Learn about cell membrane defense mechanisms.
   - Unlock shield enhancement skills.

#### Chapter 2: Genetic Code
**Learning Objective:** Basics of genetics
**Plot:** Discover a human gene bank and need to decode and repair genetic information.

**Level Design:**
1. **Puzzle: DNA Repair Station**
   - Gene sequence puzzle minigame.
   - Learn about DNA structure.
   - Unlock gene-editing tools.

2. **Stealth: Gene Bank Heist**
   - Infiltrate an enemy gene research facility.
   - Learn about gene expression regulation.
   - Obtain rare gene samples.

3. **Boss: The Mutant Behemoth**
   - Fight a giant, genetically mutated creature.
   - Apply genetic knowledge to find its weakness.
   - Obtain data on evolutionary adaptation.

#### Chapter 3: Ecosystem Restoration
**Learning Objective:** Principles of ecology
**Plot:** Rebuild the ecosystem in the wasteland and restore biodiversity.

**Level Design:**
1. **Construction: The Oasis Project**
   - Design and build an ecological park.
   - Learn about constructing food chains.
   - Balance producer and consumer ratios.

2. **Protection: Species Guardian**
   - Protect rare species from threats.
   - Learn about conservation biology.
   - Establish population protection strategies.

3. **Finale: The Cycle of Life**
   - Establish a complete, self-sustaining ecosystem.
   - Demonstrate the importance of ecological balance.
   - Set the stage for future installments in the series.

### Innovative Teaching Mechanics

#### Biology Laboratory System
- **Virtual Microscope:** Observe cell structures, learn biological morphology.
- **Gene-Editing Bench:** Simulate CRISPR operations, understand genetic engineering.
- **Petri Dish System:** Minigame for culturing microorganisms, learn growth conditions.
- **Dissection Table:** Learn through virtual dissection, understand organ functions.

#### Knowledge Verification Mechanics
- **Bio-Identification Challenge:** Identify the classification features of different organisms.
- **Evolutionary Tree Construction:** Build phylogenetic trees based on morphological and molecular evidence.
- **Ecological Network Design:** Design food webs, understand energy flow.
- **Gene Function Prediction:** Predict gene functions based on sequence characteristics.

# Core Systems Detailed Design

## Player + Vehicle Dual Stat System

### System Concept
The game uses a dual stat system separating the **Player Character** and the **Vehicle**. The player, as a biologically modified human, has independent biological attributes, while the vehicle, as mechanical equipment, has independent mechanical attributes. The two interact to form a complete combat unit.

### Player Character Stat System

#### Core Biological Attributes
**Basic Physiological Indicators**
- **Health Points (HP):** The player character's health; takes damage when the vehicle is destroyed.
- **Basal Metabolic Rate:** Affects glucose consumption speed and natural ATP recovery.
- **Neural Response Speed:** Affects the window size for "Just Frame" judgments.
- **Muscle Coordination:** Affects vehicle control precision and combo success rate.

**Bio-Energy Attributes**
- **ATP Reserve Cap:** Determines the sustainability of basic actions.
- **Hemo-Energy Capacity:** Maximum charge capacity for the main cannon system.
- **Entropy Energy Threshold:** Difficulty of triggering advanced skills.
- **Energy Conversion Efficiency:** The ratio of glucose converted to various forms of energy.

#### Biological Modification System
**Organ Enhancement**
- **Mitochondria Density:** Increases ATP recovery speed, reduces basal metabolic consumption.
- **Muscle Fiber Type:** Fast-twitch fibers for burst power, slow-twitch for endurance.
- **Neural Conduction Optimization:** Reduces skill execution complexity, improves reaction speed.
- **Circulatory System Improvement:** Speeds up hemo-energy recovery, increases "Just Frame" rewards.

**Gene Expression Regulation**
- **Adrenaline Sensitivity:** Bonus to hemo-energy gain in combat.
- **Dopamine Receptor Optimization:** Extra energy recovery on successful combos.
- **Cortisol Inhibition:** Reduces energy consumption penalty in stressful situations.
- **Growth Factor Expression:** Improves vehicle adaptability and modification compatibility.

### Vehicle Stat System

#### Mechanical Attributes
**Basic Performance**
- **Armor Value:** Damage resistance, with different armor thickness for different parts.
- **Mobility:** Movement speed, turning capability, evasion efficiency.
- **Load Capacity:** Affects weapon configuration and the number of installed modifications.
- **Energy Efficiency:** Glucose consumption multiplier for vehicle operations.

**Weapon System**
- **Main Cannon Power Coefficient:** Affects the final damage of charged attacks.
- **Secondary Cannon Fire Rate:** Combo frequency and density.
- **Ammunition Compatibility:** Number of supported special ammo types.
- **Aiming Precision:** Affects hit rate and critical hit probability.

#### Bionic Modification Attributes
**Fusion of Biological Traits**
- **Crustacean Traits:** Provides extra armor and defensive specialization.
- **Carnivore Traits:** Enhances attack power and hunting instincts.
- **Herbivore Traits:** Improves energy efficiency and escape capabilities.
- **Marine Life Traits:** Special environmental adaptation and unique attack methods.

**Adaptability System**
- **Environmental Tolerance:** Performance degradation in different terrains and climates.
- **Biocompatibility:** Degree of coordination with the player character.
- **Evolutionary Potential:** Space and direction for future upgrades.
- **Stability Index:** System reliability after complex modifications.

### Dual System Interaction Mechanism

#### Synergistic Effects
**Positive Synergy**
- Significant increase in operational precision when player's neural response matches vehicle's response speed.
- Synergy bonus when the direction of biological modification aligns with the vehicle's bionic traits.
- Optimized energy consumption when player's physique is balanced with the vehicle's load.

**Negative Conflicts**
- Increased extra consumption when player's metabolic type does not match the vehicle's energy demand.
- Decreased adaptability to the mechanical vehicle with excessive biological modification.
- Increased demand on player control with overly complex vehicle modifications.

## Unified Energy-Currency System

### Core System Philosophy
Glucose, as the only true energy source for an organism, also serves as the all-purpose in-game currency. This unifies energy consumption and economic management, giving every spending decision a biological meaning.

### Glucose Acquisition Channels

#### Primary Sources
**Combat Gains**
- Defeating enemies yields "Biomass Residue," containing unspent glucose.
- Different enemy types provide different amounts; mechanical enemies less, biological enemies more.
- Perfect combat (no damage, high combo) yields extra rewards.

**Environmental Collection**
- Plant remains, decaying food, and energy crystals in the wasteland.
- Requires biological knowledge to judge what can be safely converted.
- Some rare resources require special processing to obtain glucose.

**Production Facilities**
- Rebuilt photosynthesis facilities (greenhouses, petri dishes).
- Fermentation equipment to convert organic waste into glucose.
- Requires maintenance and upgrades, consuming other resources.

#### Special Channels
**Ecosystem Restoration**
- Establishing a complete food chain provides a continuous output of glucose.
- Output is proportional to the degree of ecological balance.
- Environmental destruction reduces output, requiring conservation awareness.

**Scientific Breakthroughs**
- Unlocking more efficient glucose utilization methods after completing biological research.
- Genetic engineering improvements to increase conversion efficiency.
- Synthetic biology techniques to artificially produce glucose.

### Glucose Consumption Mechanism

#### Energy Consumption Tiers
**Basal Metabolism (Passive Consumption)**
- Player character life support: 0.1-0.3/sec, varies with modification level.
- Vehicle basic operation: 0.05/sec at rest, 0.2-0.5/sec while moving.
- Environmental factors: Harsh environments add extra consumption.

**Active Action Consumption**
- ATP potential energy recovery: 1 Glucose = 5 ATP, instant conversion.
- Hemo-energy activation: 5-10 Glucose, halved on "Just Frame".
- Entropy skill release: 20-50 Glucose, depending on skill complexity.

**System Upgrade Consumption**
- Biological modifications: 50-500 Glucose + special materials.
- Vehicle upgrades: 30-300 Glucose + mechanical parts.
- Facility construction: 100-1000 Glucose + building materials.

#### Scarcity Design Principle
**Supply and Demand Balance**
- Difficult to obtain initially, forcing players to use every point of glucose cautiously.
- Mid-game efficiency improvements through skills and facilities.
- Late-game resource tension maintained by large-scale project demands.

**Strategic Choice Pressure**
- Use high-consumption skills to end battles quickly?
- Invest in long-term-return production facilities?
- Prioritize upgrading the player character or the vehicle?

### Economic System Design

#### Trading Mechanism
**NPC Traders**
- Shelter merchants: Basic material exchange.
- Researchers: Knowledge and technology trading.
- Wasteland scavengers: Buying and selling rare materials.

**Item Pricing Logic**
- All prices are based on glucose.
- Pricing is a composite of rarity, utility, and manufacturing cost.
- Market supply and demand affect price fluctuations.

#### Investment-Return System
**Short-term Investments**
- Buying better weapons to improve combat efficiency.
- Energy recovery items for emergencies.
- Buying information for a mission advantage.

**Long-term Investments**
- Constructing production facilities for continuous returns.
- Biological modifications to enhance base stats.
- Investing in research to unlock new technologies.

## Combat System

### Combat Philosophy
Combat is not a simple stat check but a comprehensive test of **energy management**, **timing**, and **strategic choice**. Every battle is a practical application of biological principles.

### Core Combat Loop

#### Preparation Phase
**Enemy Identification**
- Observe enemy type: Armored, Swarm, Mobile, Energy-Absorbing.
- Analyze weaknesses: Armor gaps, behavior patterns, energy signatures.
- Choose a strategy: Main cannon burst, secondary cannon sustained fire, skill breakthrough, defensive attrition.

**Energy Allocation Planning**
- Assess current glucose reserves.
- Estimate combat consumption needs.
- Formulate contingency plans.

#### Execution Phase
**Secondary Cannon Combo System**
- **Combo Mechanic:** Sustained attacks build a combo count; each combo segment restores bio-energy.
- **Rhythm Control:** The interval between hits affects recovery efficiency; too fast or too slow reduces effectiveness.
- **Target Selection:** Different enemies have different resistances to combos.
- **Charge Conversion:** Successful combos provide charge for the main cannon system.

**Main Cannon Charging System**
- **Multi-level Charging:** 1-5 charge levels, with increasing power and consumption at each level.
- **Timing Selection:** Higher charge levels require longer preparation, demanding tactical timing.
- **"Just Frame" Mechanic:** A perfect release reduces consumption by 50% and increases damage by 25%.
- **Energy Overflow:** Overcharging results in wasted energy.

**Entropy Skill Release**
- **Complex Inputs:** Requires specific button sequences or timing judgments.
- **Biological Principle:** Each skill corresponds to a biological mechanism.
- **Cooldown Mechanic:** Not time-based, but based on bio-energy accumulation.
- **Environmental Interaction:** Some skills have special reactions with the environment.

### Enemy Design Philosophy

#### Armored Enemies
**Design Concept:** Simulate heavy armored units, requiring concentrated fire to break through.
**Combat Characteristics:**
- High defense, weak against secondary cannon combos.
- Armor has weak points requiring precise aiming.
- Slow movement, giving the player time to charge.
- Sensitive to high-level main cannon charges.

**Biological Analogy:** Defensive mechanisms of crustaceans.
**Strategic Requirement:** Patient charging + precision strikes.

#### Swarm Enemies
**Design Concept:** Numerical advantage, requiring area-of-effect cleanup.
**Combat Characteristics:**
- Low individual health, but numerous.
- Highly effective against secondary cannon combos.
- Swarming tactics to limit player mobility.
- High-level main cannon charges are often overkill and wasteful.

**Biological Analogy:** Collective behavior of social animals.
**Strategic Requirement:** Combo control + area attacks.

#### Mobile Enemies
**Design Concept:** High-speed movement, difficult to hit.
**Combat Characteristics:**
- High speed, difficult to aim at.
- Varied attack patterns.
- High reliance on the shield system.
- Requires prediction and reaction skills.

**Biological Analogy:** Hunting patterns of fast predators.
**Strategic Requirement:** Predictive movement + shield defense.

#### Energy-Absorbing Enemies
**Design Concept:** Special mechanic, testing resource management.
**Combat Characteristics:**
- Drains the player's glucose reserves.
- The more energy the player has, the stronger the enemy becomes.
- Requires a quick resolution.
- Tests emergency situation handling.

**Biological Analogy:** Energy predation of parasites.
**Strategic Requirement:** Quick decision-making + resource protection.

### Shield System Mechanics

#### Trigger Conditions
**Operational Trigger**
- Perfect timing when switching between secondary and main cannons.
- A brief window after a successful "Just Frame".
- Automatic protection during specific skill releases.

**Consumption Trigger**
- Actively consume glucose to activate the shield.
- Consumption is related to shield duration and strength.
- A life-saving measure in emergencies.

#### Shield Characteristics
**Brief Invincibility**
- Duration of 0.5-2 seconds.
- Complete immunity to damage.
- Does not affect movement or attacks.

**Energy Interaction**
- Natural energy recovery stops when the shield is active.
- Consumes extra energy to maintain when hit.
- A portion of energy can be recovered by canceling it manually.

### Combat Feedback System

#### Visual Feedback
**Energy Status Display**
- Real-time display of glucose reserves.
- Visualization of ATP, Hemo-Energy, and Entropy.
- Dynamic display of combo count and charge level.
- Special effects for successful "Just Frame".

**Combat Rhythm Cues**
- Visual warnings for enemy attack patterns.
- Environmental cues for optimal attack timing.
- Display of predicted energy consumption.

#### Numerical Feedback
**Real-time Combat Data**
- Damage number display.
- Energy consumption/recovery prompts.
- Combo efficiency statistics.
- Resource utilization analysis.

**Post-Battle Summary**
- Glucose income/expenditure analysis.
- Combat efficiency evaluation.
- Suggestions for improvement.

### Combat Balance Design

#### Risk-Reward Mechanism
**High Risk, High Reward**
- "Just Frame" operations: Difficult but highly rewarding.
- High-level charges: High consumption but powerful.
- Complex skills: Difficult to execute but have unique effects.

**Resource Management Pressure**
- Glucose scarcity forces cautious decision-making.
- Different combat styles have different resource consumption rates.
- Balance between long-term strategy and short-term tactics.

#### Learning Curve Design
**Newbie-Friendly**
- Basic operations are simple and clear.
- Fault tolerance mechanisms ensure a basic experience.
- Gradual increase in complexity.

**Expert Depth**
- Infinite possibilities with complex operation combinations.
- Room for pursuing maximum efficiency.
- The joy of discovering innovative tactics.

This combat system creates a unique experience that is both educationally meaningful and deep in gameplay by merging **biological principles**, **energy management**, and **skillful operation**.
