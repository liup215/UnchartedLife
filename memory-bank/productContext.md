# Product Context: Legends of Uncharted Life

## Project Overview

**Legends of Uncharted Life** is a post-apocalyptic educational Action RPG that combines deep, strategic combat with curriculum-aligned biology education. The game aims to create an engaging learning experience where players explore unknown life forms, understand biological principles, and rebuild the miracle of life through genetic engineering and biotechnology.

## Core Pillars

### 1. Dual System Mechanics
The player controls both a biologically-modified human character and a customizable vehicle. These two entities have separate but interconnected stat systems, creating deep strategic choices in progression and combat.

### 2. Unified Energy-Currency (Glucose)
**Glucose** is the single, central resource that functions as both the player's life-sustaining energy and the game's universal currency. Every action - from combat to crafting to upgrading - has a direct, tangible cost, forcing meaningful strategic decisions rooted in biological principles of energy management.

### 3. Education Through Gameplay
Learning is not a separate mode but is woven into the core gameplay loop. Players learn about:
- **Cellular respiration** by managing their ATP
- **Genetics** by performing gene-editing
- **Ecology** by restoring the wasteland
- **Evolution** through bionic vehicle modifications

### 4. Data-Driven Modularity
The game is built on a highly modular, data-driven architecture. Core entities like enemies and weapons are defined as `Resource` files, allowing for immense variety and easy content creation.

## Core Systems

### Glucose-ATP Energy System
- Players manage glucose (universal currency) and ATP (short-term energy)
- Cellular respiration simulation for energy generation
- Sprint mechanics with tiered energy consumption
- Metabolism component handles energy calculations

### Player-Vehicle Dual System
- ON_FOOT state: Character-based exploration and combat
- IN_VEHICLE state: Tank-based combat with physics
- Shared attributes (health, ATP) but separate combat systems
- Seamless transitions between states

### Combat System
- Unified weapon system for both actors and vehicles
- Charge mechanics (1-5 levels) with ATP costs
- Combo systems for sustained attacks
- Visual feedback (damage numbers, muzzle effects)
- BioBlitz mode: Combat pauses for biology questions when enemy health is low

### Biology Knowledge Integration
- **Cell Biology**: Understanding organelles, cellular respiration, ATP generation
- **Genetics**: DNA structure, gene expression, CRISPR technology
- **Ecology**: Food chains, ecosystem restoration, biodiversity
- **Evolution**: Natural selection, adaptive traits, bionic modifications

## Target Audience
- High school biology students
- Science enthusiasts
- Players interested in educational games with strategic depth
- Teachers seeking engaging biology learning tools

## Educational Goals
Help students move from "knowing biology concepts" to "understanding why biological systems work the way they do" through hands-on gameplay experience.
